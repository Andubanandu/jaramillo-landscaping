Why Jaramillo Landscape?

Most sprinkler problems aren't fixed by just swapping the obvious part — they have a root cause that keeps the problem coming back. We diagnose first, then repair. That approach means fewer repeat service calls and a system that actually works reliably.

We service all major brands — Rain Bird, Hunter, Rainbird, Toro, Orbit, Irritrol, and others. We carry a full stock of common replacement heads, nozzles, and parts in our service vehicles so most repairs are completed on the first visit.

We're straightforward about what we find. If a repair isn't cost-effective compared to replacement, we'll tell you honestly and explain your options before doing any work.

Our Process

We run the full system first — you can't find everything by looking at one zone at a time.

1

Full System Run-Through

We run every zone and document what we find — broken heads, coverage gaps, stuck zones, or any other issues — before touching anything.

2

Diagnosis & Review

We identify the root cause of each issue and review our findings with you before starting repairs so there are no surprises.

3

Repair & Replacement

Heads, nozzles, valves, and other components are replaced with compatible quality parts. We don't use the cheapest parts available.

4

Re-Test All Zones

After repairs, we run every zone again to verify coverage and confirm all issues are resolved before closing up.

5

Head Adjustment & Coverage Check

We adjust all heads for proper arc and distance, ensuring complete coverage with minimal overspray onto hard surfaces.

Typical Timeframe

Same Day

Most sprinkler repairs are completed in a single visit. Complex repairs or parts that must be ordered may require a follow-up appointment within 1–2 days.

A zone that won't shut off is almost always caused by a faulty solenoid or debris stuck in the valve diaphragm. Sometimes it's a wiring issue sending a constant signal to the valve. We diagnose the exact cause before replacing any parts — the fix is usually quick once the root cause is identified.

We recommend a spring startup inspection every year to check heads, coverage, pressure, and controller programming. Catching small problems early — a slightly tilted head, a clogged nozzle — prevents them from becoming water-wasting or plant-killing problems by mid-summer.
