What We Remove

We handle complete yard removals or targeted removal of specific materials — whatever your project requires.

  • Sod & lawn removal
  • Dead or unwanted plants & shrubs
  • Old gravel, rock & decomposed granite
  • Wood chips & mulch
  • Yard debris & organic material
  • Old weed fabric & landscape edging
  • Light grading after removal
  • Complete haul-away & disposal

Why Jaramillo Landscape?

Yard removal is hard, heavy work — and disposing of materials properly is its own challenge. We bring the right equipment and enough crew to remove material efficiently without damaging what you're keeping, and we handle disposal so you don't have to arrange trucks or dump runs.

We're especially useful before a new landscape installation. Starting fresh from bare soil means your new irrigation, plants, and hardscape will be installed on a clean, level surface — not over old sod roots or compacted gravel that wasn't meant to be there.

We mark and protect anything you want to keep — existing trees, irrigation lines, valued plants — before any removal begins.

Our Process

Efficient, thorough, and careful about what you're keeping.

1

Walk-Through & Planning

We walk the property with you, mark what stays and what goes, and identify anything to protect (irrigation lines, trees, valued plants).

2

Material Removal

Sod, gravel, plants, and debris are systematically removed starting with the heaviest material first.

3

Loading & Haul-Away

All removed material is loaded into our trucks and hauled off-site for disposal. We don't leave anything behind.

4

Light Grading

After removal, we lightly grade the area to level low spots and create a clean, even surface ready for whatever comes next.

5

Final Inspection

We walk the area with you to confirm everything is removed as agreed and the surface is ready for your next project.

Typical Timeframe

1–2 Days

Most yard removals are complete in a single day. Large properties or heavy material volumes may require a second day or additional haul trips.

Cost Factors

  • Total area to be removed
  • Material type & weight (gravel vs. sod)
  • Volume of haul-away material
  • Equipment access
  • Depth of material (thick gravel beds)
