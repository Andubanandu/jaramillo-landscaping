What's Included

A retaining wall built to last starts underground — proper footing, drainage, and batter are what keeps walls standing for decades.

  • Site assessment & wall design
  • Excavation & base trench preparation
  • Compacted gravel base course
  • Block, stone, or boulder wall construction
  • Drainage aggregate behind wall
  • Geogrid reinforcement where required
  • Backfill & compaction
  • Cap course & finish work

Why Jaramillo Landscape?

A retaining wall that fails is worse than no wall at all — it becomes a safety hazard and an expensive problem to fix. We build walls the right way the first time: proper batter (backward lean), drainage behind the wall to prevent hydrostatic pressure, and a solid compacted footing that won't shift.

We work with all wall materials: Allan Block, Versa-Lok, natural stone, large boulders, and dry-stack designs. We'll help you choose the material that fits your aesthetic, your budget, and the engineering requirements of your site.

Walls over 4 feet may require engineering review under local code. We know the regulations and will advise you on any permitting requirements during the estimate.

Our Process

Every wall starts below ground — the quality of your footing determines how long the wall stands.

1

Site Assessment & Wall Design

We assess the slope, soil conditions, drainage needs, and load requirements to design a wall that will hold safely for decades.

2

Excavation & Base Preparation

We excavate the footing trench to below frost depth and prepare a level, compacted gravel base for the first course.

3

First Course & Alignment

The base course is set level and aligned precisely — this determines the straightness and stability of every course above it.

4

Wall Construction with Drainage

Each course is set with proper batter (setback), and drainage aggregate is installed behind the wall as we go to prevent water pressure buildup.

5

Backfill & Compaction

Soil is backfilled in lifts and compacted layer by layer to prevent settling behind the wall.

6

Cap Course & Final Finish

The cap course is set with adhesive for a clean, finished look. The site is cleaned up and restored before we leave.

Typical Timeframe

2–7 Days

Short garden walls (under 3 feet) are typically complete in 2–3 days. Longer or taller walls may take a full week. We'll give you a clear timeline with your estimate.
